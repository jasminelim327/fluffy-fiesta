Getting started in 3 steps

  • 1
    Open the bot and send /start. Fluffy Fiesta will introduce itself and ask you to set your first daily habit. This is the foundation everything else is built on.
  • 2
    Share your location once (tap 📎 → Location in Telegram) so the bot knows your timezone and can send morning briefs and reminders at the right local time.
  • 3
    Set your daily habit. Type something like "15 min reading every day" or "30 min workout daily". From that moment, Fluffy Fiesta tracks your streak automatically.
Tip: You can also set your timezone by typing "my timezone is Singapore" (or any city name) — no location share needed.

Capture tasks and reminders

Just describe what you need to do — no special syntax required. The bot extracts the action, deadline, and priority automatically.

"Call dentist on Friday"
Saves as a task with a Friday deadline
"Submit report by Monday 9am"
Saves with a timed deadline — reminder fires at 9am Monday
"Buy groceries today"
Saves as high-priority for today
"Team standup every day at 9am"
Creates a recurring daily reminder

Tasks with a specific time (e.g. "at 3pm") get a reminder message with ✅ Done and ⏰ Snooze 30min buttons. Tasks without a time are saved but only shown in your list — no reminder fires.

To view tasks: tap 📋 My Tasks or send /tasks. Tap ✅ next to any task to mark it done, or ⏰ to snooze it by 30 minutes.

Build a daily habit streak

A habit is a single daily commitment you want to make non-negotiable. You set it once; the bot tracks it every day.

  • 1
    Set your habit: type "15 min reading every day" or "30 min yoga daily". The bot confirms and starts tracking from today.
  • 2
    At 8pm you get a nudge asking if you did it. Tap ✅ I did it to log progress — or just type "I did it" or "done" at any time.
  • 3
    Check your streak with /streak or the 🔥 button. After 4+ days, the bot shows a week view and a data-driven line about your strongest and weakest days.
Why one habit? Research consistently shows that trying to build multiple habits at once dramatically reduces success rates. Pick the one thing that, if done daily, would have the biggest positive impact on your work. Add others once the first is solid.

Log energy — unlock your peak hours

Energy logging is the most underused feature. It takes 2 seconds and unlocks genuinely useful scheduling insight after about a week.

How to log: just send a number from 1 to 10 at any point in the day. Once is fine; multiple times is better.

3
Low energy — logs it, shows week average, and offers a motivation boost
8
High energy — suggests tackling something hard right now
/energy
Opens the energy prompt if you can't remember the scale

After 7 check-ins the bot tells you your peak energy time (e.g. "Tuesday mornings"). After that, it shows your actual best time with each new log. This is when to schedule your hardest work — deep focus, creative output, complex decisions.

Pro move: log your energy at the same time each day — say, right after lunch and before bed. Consistent timing makes the patterns much more meaningful.

Understand your patterns

Two commands give you different views of your data:

/patterns
How you work — behavioural analysis based on task history, energy logs, and habit streaks. Needs 5+ energy logs and 3+ habit check-ins. Tells you what trips you up and one thing to try this week.
/review
This week's review — what happened in the past 7 days: habit completions, tasks finished, energy average. Needs 3+ days of check-ins. A backward-looking weekly summary.
Think of it this way: /review tells you what happened. /patterns tells you why and how to improve.

If either shows "not enough data yet", it tells you exactly what's missing — energy logs, habit check-ins, or both. Just keep your daily routine and the insight builds up automatically.

Set long-term goals with milestones

Long-term goals are for bigger ambitions that take weeks or months. The bot breaks them into 4–5 concrete milestones and tracks your progress with a visual progress bar.

  • 1
    Say something like "I want to build a SaaS product" or use /longterm. The bot asks for the goal name, your reason for doing it, and a timeline.
  • 2
    It proposes 4–5 milestones. You can say "yes" to accept or tell it what to change — it'll revise and re-propose.
  • 3
    Use /longterm to see your goals with progress bars. Tap 📍 on any goal to see milestones with ✅ Done buttons.
  • 4
    When you complete a milestone, tap ✅ or type something like "I finished the MVP" — the bot matches it to the right milestone automatically.

Pro tips for maximum productivity

One habit at a time. Set a single daily commitment and keep it for at least 30 days before adding another. Consistency compounds faster than variety.
Log energy at consistent times. Morning and evening readings give the most useful patterns. Seven days of consistent logging unlocks your personal peak-hour schedule.
Check /streak every morning. Seeing your week view first thing — even before opening other apps — makes it harder to let the day slip by without doing your habit.
Use /coach after a tough week. The coaching moment analyses your actual data (streak, energy average, tasks, goals) and gives you a specific, honest reflection — not generic advice.
Say "I finished the MVP" instead of tapping manually. The bot understands natural milestone completion phrases and matches them to your goal automatically.
Connect Google Calendar. Use /connect to link your Google Calendar. New tasks with deadlines are added automatically, and today's events appear in your task list alongside your bot tasks.
Ask it anything. Fluffy Fiesta has access to your task history, habit data, energy logs, and goals — so questions like "what should I focus on today?" or "am I on track with my reading goal?" get genuinely contextual answers, not generic ones.
